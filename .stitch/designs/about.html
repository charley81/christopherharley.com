Christopher Harley

I’m a creative technologist who fell into web development while learning to code and stayed for the design. What started as curiosity about how websites worked quickly became a fascination with how they felt — the whitespace, the rhythm, the subtle details that make something feel right. That pull toward the visual side led me deep into UI/UX design, and for the last six years I’ve been moving between design and development, always chasing that intersection where beauty meets function.

Beyond the screen, my work is heavily influenced by the compositional rigor of electronic music and minimalist editorial design. I believe that removing visual noise allows the true intent of an interface to emerge, transforming functional tools into quiet, confident experiences.

Currently exploring the intersection of generative models and precise layout systems, striving to build interfaces that feel engineered rather than merely decorated.
